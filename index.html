Investment Thesis
Executive Summary
The Yarotek Portfolio is an 82 MWdc utility-scale solar asset in Hoke and Robeson Counties, North Carolina, interconnected to Duke Energy Carolinas. At the asking price of $110M, the asset trades at a 13% discount to our base-case DCF value of $126.5M. The project has a confirmed 5-year PPA with Duke at $59/MWh and a 40% ITC confirmed via Safe Harbor. Three medium-severity engineering issues — soiling assumptions, an inverter change notification to Duke, and transformer delivery timing — require resolution before close.
Technical Assessment
PVsyst P50 of 1,710 kWh/kWp validated against EIA benchmarks for the project location. Performance ratio of 81.5% is within normal range for fixed-tilt single-axis tracker in SERC. The inverter model change from SG3125 to SG3600 has not been disclosed to Duke Energy — this must be filed immediately to avoid triggering a re-study that could delay COD by 4–8 months. GSU transformer delivery is 2 weeks behind schedule; recommend confirming expedite option before close.
Financial Assessment
Base-case levered IRR of 12.9% pre-tax exceeds our 10.0% hurdle rate by 290 bps. The PPA expires in Year 5 (2034), after which revenue shifts to merchant/Aurora pricing at $65/MWh escalating at 1.5%/yr. This transition is the primary revenue risk. DSCR floors at 1.62x in Year 1, well above the 1.25x covenant. The 40% ITC generates approximately $57M in tax credit transfer proceeds at COD, significantly reducing required equity capital.
Recommendation
Proceed with a bid of $96.25M — 12.5% below asking price — contingent on: (1) EPC filing inverter change notification with Duke Energy before signing; (2) GSU transformer delivery confirmation by June 2026; (3) seller providing a PPA renewal right or right-of-first-refusal for the post-2034 merchant period. At the recommended bid price, levered IRR improves to approximately 14.2%.
